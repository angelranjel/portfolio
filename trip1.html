Exploring Oregon's Pacific Coast

During our 10-day trip to Oregon we got to see some of the most breathtaking views. Our trip began on the Pacific Coast east of Portland. We started out at Ecola State Park and walked for miles on the sandy beaches and rocky shores. This was my second time seeing the Pacific Ocean up close and it never ceases to amaze me. Just south of Ecola is the famous Cannon Beach with its iconic Haystack Rock like a castle on the beach. We decided to stay and watch the sun set as little fires begin to spring up all over Cannon Beach. We spent most of our trip wandering up and down the Pacific coast, but we did make our way inland to Crater Lake National Park and Mount Hood. Eventually we found our way back north towards the Oregon-Washington border naturally separated by the Columbia River Gorge. Other places of note we visited was the Oneonta River Gorge and Waterfall, Spirit Falls, and Portland.
