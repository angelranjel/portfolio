The Road From Salt Lake City to Olympic

My brothers and I came up with a plan to travel throughout the Great Northwest and visit every national park that we possibly can. We landed in Salt Lake City, Utah and drove north towards the Grand Tetons National Park in Wyoming. From there we made our way towards the popular Yellowstone National Park. There we visited the hot springs and captured photographs of wild bison herds running through the prairie. We continued our way north towards Montana and near the border of Canada we stopped at Glacier National Park. I am convinced that Glacier National Park is one of the most beautiful places in the world. The turquoise blue lakes and rivers, nestled in the valleys full of wildlife, between towering mountains appears otherworldly to me. After Glacier we moved on to the state of Washington where we visited Mount Rainier. From there we drove up to Olympic National Park west of Seattle. At Olympic we visited the tide pools at the edge of the park and ventured into the ancient Hoh Rain Forest.
